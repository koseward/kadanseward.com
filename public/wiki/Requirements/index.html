Requirements

Overview

Requirements, also known as user requirements, or software requirements are descriptions of the functionality of a given system. There are two primary types of requirements: functional and non-functional requirements. The former are what the system should do - whether it searches through items, does an operation, or completes a task. Non-functional requirements describe how a system completes the functional requirements. These are wide ranging, from security requirements, to how an app will communicate with outside structures.

Relation to Software Architecture

Requirements are an important part of choosing an appropriate architecture. A set of requirements are necessary before any choice can be made, as that list informs which characteristics are important for the system to have. Then a decision can be made as to what architecture is most effective.
